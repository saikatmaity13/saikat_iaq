Why Indoor Air Quality Matters

Health Impacts of Poor IAQ:

Respiratory diseases

Chronic exposure to indoor pollutants can lead to asthma, bronchitis, and other respiratory conditions.

Cardiovascular problems

Poor air quality is linked to increased risk of heart disease and stroke.

Cognitive impairment

Studies show air pollution can affect cognitive function and productivity.

Common Indoor Pollutants:

  • Particulate matter (PM10/PM2.5): Tiny particles that can penetrate deep into lungs
  • Carbon dioxide (CO₂): High levels indicate poor ventilation
  • Nitrogen dioxide (NO₂): Produced by combustion processes
  • Volatile organic compounds (VOCs): Emitted from paints, cleaning supplies, and furnishings
